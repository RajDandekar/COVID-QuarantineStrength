Machine Learning aided Epidemiology: COVID-19 Global quarantine strength and reproduction number evolution

The quarantine strength function and the effective reproduction variation in several countries is estimated. The method followed is based on augmentation of the standard SIR epidemiological model with machine learning. Our model is universally applicable making it a flexible and powerful tool to analyze and compare the efficacy of government measures in curtailing virus spread in different countries. This work is an extension of the MedrRxiv article published on 1 April 2020.

Data is collected from Center for Systems Science and Engineering (CSSE) at Johns Hopkins University.

Additional Notes

For the United States, since the recovered data is not as reliable as the number of deaths, the R in the SIR model is the number of dead people who can no longer transmit infection. For all other regions, R is the number of "recovered + dead" people, who can no longer transmit infection.
